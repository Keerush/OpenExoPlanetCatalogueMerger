System Validations

{{desc}}

Throughout our entire project, we were able to gather user requirements, create user stories, create sprint plans, organize our tasks using a taskboard, implement our features using a version control system, and finally test our implementations. During deliverables 3 and 4, we were able to implement several of our user stories. We also faced the issue of having to re-plan several of our sprints and having to re-order which user stories to implement first. We experienced what it was like to test our implementations and then improve on them after code reviews. During deliverable 5, most of our planning were refined and we managed to smoothly progress according to our sprint plans. In the end we managed to implement the user stories that we believed were of highest priority, leaving behind several features that we weren't as important, but would have been nice to implement when given more time.

For sprint 5, our estimated project velocity was 36. Our actual was 32. For sprint 6, our estimated was 22. Our actual was 22. For sprint 7, our estimated was 22. Our actual was 16.

Overall, we felt that in this deliverable we were able to follow most of our plans. Other than the minor changes we made in our product backlog, no other re-plans were made during our sprints. Judging from our project velocity, we were effective with our time except for our seventh sprint where there were days when we weren't able to commit to our plans.

Overall, the main difference between deliverable 5 and all our other deliverables was that the progress we made was a lot smoother. We believe this is the case because as we progressed further with our sprints, most of the necessary re-planning has already been made during our earlier sprints. As a result, most of the features we planned on releasing during deliverable 5 were met, whereas we weren't able to release several features during the previous deliverables.
